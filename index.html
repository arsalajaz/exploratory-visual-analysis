Initial Analysis Questions

  1. Does age have any relations with jovilaity and does having kids play a role in it?
  2. Is there any relationship between Money people spend on food, money they have avialable, and jovilaity?
  3. Does home location play a role in people's level of happiness? Why are people living in the outskirts of the city are not as happy as the people living in the center?
  4. Looking at the average age of those with a specfic educational background, does having kids have anything to do with it? What about their educational background and jovilality?

Discoveries & Insights

Age vs average joviality trend

This visualization shows that as the participants age, their overall level of happiness tends to decline. Having children as a factor changes things slightly, as those in their 20s seem to be happier without children. Those who are well into their adulthood (40+) seem to be thankful to have children, in comparison.

Avg Available balance and avg food budget vs joviality trend

These graph describe the relationship between Average Avaialble balance, Average food budget and jovialiy. We graphed these two dimesnion on same page to see if there is any relationship between. It show that people who have less available balance and have higher budget for food, tend to be more jovial.

We used the participant logs table to find out where people lived and combined that with particiipants table to show thier joviality. I was a little suprised about the fact that few participants logged "AtHome" status from different locations. The overall visualization shows that the people on the edges of the town are not as happy as people living in the middle.

Average joviality vs educational background (with and w/o kids)

In this visualization, we can see that those with a graduate education are the most happy overall. If you view the interactive and contrasting data, you can see that those with a lower education and have kids are the most happy, which is quite a surprise. The least happy are those with no kids and a lower education.

The visualization above depicts the cost of rent depending on the area of the city. The darker shade of red indicates higher rent while lighter is lower. We were suprised to not see a specfic pattern in the cost of rent. It seems uniform across the city.

Summary

In this data analysis, we explored several trends in the city of Engagement, Ohio, and the participants in the survey related to their overall happiness (joviality). In general, it appears that those who are older are less happy, and younger people without kids are the most happy. People in their 20s with children seem to be not that happy, but they seem to be thankful to have children in their 40s. In terms of who can buy the best food, those who are able to spend the most daily are the most happy, whereas people who have more available to spend but spend less are less happy. For those with lower education, surprisingly, the ones with kids are the happiest, while those with the lowest education without kids are the least happy. Finally, we tried to correlate rent price, living location, and joviality by creating two maps. We have found that overall it does not seem like those who pay a higher amount of rent are happier.
